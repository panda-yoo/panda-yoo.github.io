
# Newton's Law of Motion for a Tagged Particle

$$
m \frac{d \mathbf{v}(t)}{dt} = \mathbf{F}(t) \rightarrow \mathbf{F}(t) = \text{total force}
$$

$$
\mathbf{F}(t) = \mathbf{F}_{\text{int}}(t) + \mathbf{F}_{\text{ext}}(t)
$$

- $\mathbf{F}_{\text{ext}}(t)$: all external force / applied forces
- $\mathbf{F}_{\text{int}}$: arising from boundaries or order
  - $\mathbf{F}_{\text{int}}$ is a random force

* It turns out internal consistency requires $\mathbf{F}_{\text{int}}$ to be made of 2 distinct parts

$$
\mathbf{F}_{\text{int}}(t) = \mathbf{\eta}(t) + \mathbf{F}_{\text{sys}}(t)
$$

1. $<\mathbf{\eta}(t)> = 0 \rightarrow$ truly random force / noise
   - and is independent of the state of the motion of the tagged particle

2. $\mathbf{F}_{\text{sys}}(t) \rightarrow$ systematic random force that depends on
   - state of tagged particle

* Simplest assumption for $\mathbf{F}_{\text{sys}}(t)$ is proportional to instantaneous velocity of tagged particle and is directed opposite direction

$$
\mathbf{F}_{\text{sys}}(t) = -m \gamma \mathbf{v}(t)
$$

- $\gamma \rightarrow$ positive friction coefficient
